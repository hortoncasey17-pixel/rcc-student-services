Residency Determination

NC residency affects your tuition rate — here is what you need to know.

info_outlineRCC is not involved in the residency determination process. Contact RDS directly with all questions.

NC residency status determines whether you pay in-state or out-of-state tuition. To qualify as an in-state student, you or your parent or guardian must have established legal domicile in North Carolina for at least 12 consecutive months before the term begins.

RCC uses the NC Residency Determination Service (RDS) to confirm residency. Note: RCC is not involved in the determination process — all decisions are made by RDS directly.

looks_oneGo to ncresidency.org and create an account.
looks_twoComplete your Residency Certification Number (RCN) application.
looks_3Submit your RCN with your RCC admissions application.
looks_4RDS reviews and notifies you of your status.

All documents must show 12 or more months of NC domicile.

If RDS determines you do not qualify for in-state tuition, you have the right to appeal. Gather documents showing your NC address, submit everything RDS requests as quickly as possible, and keep copies of everything you send.

Active duty military members stationed in NC under military orders, and their dependents, may qualify for in-state tuition rates. Contact RDS directly for guidance on your specific situation.

phoneContact RDS — 844.319.3640

Under NC law, DACA and undocumented students are generally classified as non-residents for tuition purposes. However, colleges may offer institutional support, scholarships, or other funding opportunities. We encourage you to speak confidentially with an academic advisor.
